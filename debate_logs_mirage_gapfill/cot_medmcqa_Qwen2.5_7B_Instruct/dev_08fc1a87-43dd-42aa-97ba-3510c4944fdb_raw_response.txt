The stain used is Triphenyl tetrazolium chloride (TTC), which reduces to formazan under hypoxic conditions indicating viable vs non-viable tissue. In an acute MI, ischemic myocardium would not have sufficient oxygen for this reduction reaction leading to no uptake or light brown staining post fixation, while normal tissue takes up TTC and appears red-brown.
Given that the area of interest had been damaged by an MI, it wouldn't show any uptake of TTC meaning it should appear white upon examination after being fixed and treated with the dye.
