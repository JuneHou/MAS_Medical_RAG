The dried blood spots (DBS) test, also known as Guthrie test or heel prick test, is commonly utilized to screen newborns for various metabolic disorders that can be life-threatening if not detected early. This non-invasive method involves collecting small amounts of blood from the baby's heel onto special cards which are then analyzed for specific biochemical markers indicative of these conditions. 

The correct option is A: Inborn errors of metabolism. DBS tests are primarily employed to identify inherited metabolic diseases such as phenylketonuria (PKU), hypothyroidism, galactosemia, among others, by measuring levels of certain substances in the serum obtained through this simple procedure.
