The key pathophysiological feature of hyaline membrane disease (HMD), often seen in preterm infants, involves underdeveloped alveoli that do not produce adequate surfactant. This leads to decreased surface tension within the alveoli which can cause collapse during exhalation if the functional residual capacity (FRC) does not exceed the critical closing volume necessary for maintaining open alveoli. Therefore, HMD is typically associated with an FRC less than the closing volume, leading to frequent re-collapse of alveoli post-exhalation.

Answer Choice: A Your Answer: **A** The Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) is smaller than the closing volume in cases of hyaline membrane disease due to inadequate surfactant production resulting in increased risk of alveolar collapse.
