In patients with acute or chronic hepatitis B, the presence of surface antigen (HBsAg) is typically indicative of an active viral replication as it represents the outer coat protein that signals the immune system to respond against this virus. The core antigen (HBC Ag), while present during the early stages of infection, is usually not detectable by standard serological tests due to its location within the hepatocytes. Immune responses generate antibodies directed against both HBV antigens; however, anti-HBs antibody production indicates past exposure or immunity from vaccination rather than current viremia, whereas anti-HBc can indicate either recent or historical infection but does not specifically indicate ongoing disease activity.

The correct marker for active Hepatitis B infection would thus be:

**Answer A: HbsAg**

This marker directly signifies the presence of circulating virally infected cells and hence reflects current infectivity and viral load. **(Final Answer)** To confirm my response, I'd also consider any other relevant clinical data such as liver function tests and DNA quantification if necessary.
