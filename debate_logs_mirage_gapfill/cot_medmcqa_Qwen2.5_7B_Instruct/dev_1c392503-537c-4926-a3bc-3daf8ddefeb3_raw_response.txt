The absolute indications for open reduction in condylar fractures include significant lateral or anterior-posterior displacements that cannot be corrected by closed methods. This ensures proper alignment and function post-reduction, which is crucial for mandibular integrity and occlusion.

My answer is B: Lateral displacement of the condyle (or) Lateral fracture-dislocation condyle. Anatomical repositioning under direct visualization through an open approach is necessary to achieve optimal healing when there's substantial lateral misalignment that would impede functional recovery if left untreated.
