I largely concur with the assessment but would emphasize another aspect. In addition to the existing reasons provided by others, it’s crucial to consider that hypernatremic children on strict breastfeeding regimens can sometimes have issues related to their mother’s diet if she has undiagnosed thyroid disorders affecting her milk composition. This could also contribute to hypernatremia despite being primarily attributed to DI. Thus, while diabetes insipidus seems highly likely based on current data, a thorough maternal history review is important too.
