When dealing with an infected or abscessed tooth that has led to a sinus tract formation (indicating periapical pathology), it is crucial to treat both the source of infection within the root canal system and address any existing sinus tract. Treating only one without addressing the other could result in incomplete resolution and potential recurrence of symptoms such as pain, swelling, and discharge from the sinus opening.

The appropriate course would involve thorough debridement and possibly drainage if necessary via curettage of the sinus tract to ensure all infectious material is removed before further management steps like obturation can be performed effectively.

My Answer: **C** - Curettage of sinus To remove any residual inflammatory tissue or debris that may still reside in the sinus tract after endodontic therapy. This ensures optimal healing conditions for successful long-term outcomes post-treatment.
