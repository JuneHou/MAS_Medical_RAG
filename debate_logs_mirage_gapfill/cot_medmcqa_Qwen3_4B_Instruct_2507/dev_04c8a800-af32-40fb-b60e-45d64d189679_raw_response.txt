**Answer:** C: Class III  

Clinical reasoning: Graft placement is typically successful in classes I, II, and IV recurrences due to predictable tissue availability. In Class III recession, there is significant loss of connective tissue with exposed root surfaces, making it difficult for grafts to integrate properly or achieve adequate support—leading to higher risk of failure despite surgical intervention. Thus, Class III represents the threshold where success rates drop significantly compared to other classifications.
