**Answer:** D: Phosphoglucomutase  

Phosphoglucomutase catalyzes the interconversion of glucose-6-phosphate and glucose-1-phosphate, which occurs during both glycogenesis (when glucose-6-P is converted to glucose-1-P for glycosidic bond formation) and glycogenolysis (where glucose-1-P is generated from glycogen breakdown). This shared enzymatic step makes it the only enzyme present in both pathways. The other enzymes—glycogen synthase, glycogen phosphorylase, and glucan transferase—are specific to one pathway or not directly involved in both processes. Therefore, phosphoglucomutase is uniquely common to both reactions.
