**Answer:** C: scarlet fever  

*Clinical reasoning:* "Strawberry tongue" refers to a bright red, raised, bumpy appearance of the tongue due to engorged papillae, commonly seen in children. This finding is classically associated with scarlet fever caused by group A streptococcus (Streptococcus pyogenes), which also presents with rash, sore throat, and fever. While other conditions like measles or syphilis may have oral manifestations, strawberry tongue is not typically characteristic of those diseases. Typhoid usually does not present with this sign. Therefore, the best association remains scarlet fever.
