The orthodontic treatment typically creates new space between teeth, which can be prone to closing over time if not properly maintained. Retainers play an essential role after orthodontic alignment or tooth movement because they help stabilize newly placed teeth into their correct positions until bone remodeling occurs around them.

My answer is C: Bonded lingual retainers provide constant retention against forces that might cause relapse such as tongue thrusting and jaw movements while being less visible than traditional Hawleys.
