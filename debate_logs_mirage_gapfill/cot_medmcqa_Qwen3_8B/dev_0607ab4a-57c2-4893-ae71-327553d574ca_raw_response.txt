**Answer:** A: Ameloblastoma  
**Clinical Reasoning:** Dentigerous cysts are commonly associated with odontogenic tumors, particularly ameloblastomas, due to their origin from dental epithelial tissues. While other lesions may arise near impacted teeth, there is no direct causal link between dentigerous cyst formation and adenocarcinoma or fibrosarcoma; thus, only ameloblastoma is clinically supported as a related neoplasm.
