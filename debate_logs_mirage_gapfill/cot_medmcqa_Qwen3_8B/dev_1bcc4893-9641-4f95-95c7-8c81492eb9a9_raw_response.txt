**Answer:** A: Irreversible hydrocolloid  
Irreversible hydrocolloids, such as alginate, exhibit hysteresis due to their viscoelastic properties—when they set, there is an irreversible change in volume (shrinkage), leading to a lag between deformation and recovery. This characteristic makes them prone to dimensional inaccuracies compared to reversible materials that return fully upon removal of stress.
