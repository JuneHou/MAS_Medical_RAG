**Answer:** B: Wetting the periphery of the impression with moist cotton to break the peripheral seal  

Clinical reasoning: Hydrocolloid impressions adhere strongly due to their gel-like consistency, so gently wetting the perimeter helps dissolve the adhesive bond without causing trauma. This method allows for controlled release while minimizing tissue irritation or damage to the impression material.
