**Answer:** C: Increased secondary radiation fog to film  

**Reasoning:** The grid absorbs some primary radiation, but also increases scatter radiation that reaches the image receptor, leading to greater secondary radiation fog on the film. This reduces contrast and requires higher mAs to compensate, increasing overall patient dose indirectly.
