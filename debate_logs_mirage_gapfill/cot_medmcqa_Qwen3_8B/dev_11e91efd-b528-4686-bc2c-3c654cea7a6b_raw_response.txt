**Answer:** A: Max. Lateral incisor  

**Clinical Reasoning:** Microdontia is most frequently observed in the maxillary lateral incisors, which are small due to developmental factors such as reduced enamel formation or abnormal odontogenesis. This finding is well-documented in dental literature and represents one of the more common instances of microdontia among permanent teeth.
