The patient's symptoms (sore throat, headache, vomiting), hypotension (low BP), and petechiae suggest an infectious process affecting multiple organ systems. Given her age and presentation, meningococcemia is highly likely due to its classic triad of fever, severe systemic toxicity, and skin manifestations like petechiae or purpura associated with N. meningitidis infection. 

Answer Choice B: Neisseria Meningitidis To support this diagnosis further, what additional findings would you expect during physical examination? Additional expected findings for suspected meningococcemia include high fever, tachycardia, cold extremities despite normal central body temperature ("hypothermic peripheries"), conjunctival injection, and possibly more extensive rash including ecchymoses. Capillary refill may be prolonged indicating poor perfusion. Laboratory tests such as thrombocytopenia, elevated lactate levels, and positive Gram stain/culture on blood samples from sepsis cultures can also confirm the suspicion.
