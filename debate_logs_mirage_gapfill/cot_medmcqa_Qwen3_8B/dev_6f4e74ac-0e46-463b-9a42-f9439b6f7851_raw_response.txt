Hyperparathyroidism leads to increased activity of the parathyroid hormone (PTH), which stimulates bone resorption, increasing serum calcium. This hormonal imbalance can also lead to enhanced urinary phosphate wasting as PTH increases tubular reabsorption of sodium while decreasing that of phosphate. 

The correct option here would be D: Increased loss of phosphate in urine. Clinical Reasoning: In hyperparathyroidism, elevated PTH levels cause an increase in the filtration rate through the kidneys leading to more phosphate being lost into the urine.
