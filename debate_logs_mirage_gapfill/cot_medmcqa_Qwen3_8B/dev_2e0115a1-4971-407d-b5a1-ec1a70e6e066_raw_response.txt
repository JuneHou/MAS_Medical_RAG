**D**: Integration of viral DNA with host DNA  

Asymptomatic hepatitis B infection can lead to chronic liver disease due to persistent viral presence; integration of HBV DNA into the host genome disrupts cellular regulatory mechanisms (e.g., tumor suppressor genes), promoting uncontrolled cell proliferation and increasing the risk of hepatocellular carcinoma over time. This genomic instability is a key driver in carcinogenesis even without overt symptoms or elevated transaminases.
