**Answer:** A: Middle Third  
**Clinical Reasoning:** Accessory canals are more commonly found in the apical third due to complex root anatomy. However, studies show that the middle third also harbors significant numbers of accessory canals, particularly in multi-rooted teeth like molars or premolars where canal branching occurs throughout the root length. Thus, while the apical third has the highest concentration, the middle third ranks as second-most common location for accessory canals.
