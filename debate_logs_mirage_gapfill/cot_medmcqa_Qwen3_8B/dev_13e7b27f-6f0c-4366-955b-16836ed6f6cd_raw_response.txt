**Answer:** B: Citrate  

**Clinical Reasoning:** Citrate-based anticoagulants bind calcium, which prevents clotting while preserving serum electrolytes. This makes citrated blood suitable for laboratory testing that requires accurate measurement of ions like potassium, sodium, chloride, and magnesium—commonly assessed in routine electrolyte panels. Other options (EDTA, lithium heparin, or sodium fluoride) either interfere with ion measurements or are not typically used for standard electrolyte estimations. Thus, citrate is ideal when evaluating serum electrolytes.
