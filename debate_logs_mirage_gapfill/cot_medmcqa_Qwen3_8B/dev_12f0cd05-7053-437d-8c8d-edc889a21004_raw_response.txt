Cardiac tamponade is characterized by reduced venous return due to compressive effects on the heart from fluid accumulation around it. Rapid or deep breathing can increase intrathoracic pressures which may worsen symptoms such as hypotension and dyspnea. Therefore, normal breathing would be advisable during measurement of blood pressure for this condition.

Answer: **B** (Breathe normally)
In my clinical experience, guiding patients with cardiac tamponade to breathe naturally helps avoid exacerbating their already compromised hemodynamics through changes in thoracic pressure that could interfere with accurate BP readings. Deep or rapid breathing might transiently improve some measures but risk worsening overall circulatory status in these critically ill individuals. Normal respiration allows stable conditions necessary for reliable monitoring.
