**Answer:** B: Zone of infection  

**Reasoning:** The zone of infection is where bacterial toxins, including bacteriocidal ones (such as those produced by *Staphylococcus aureus* or *Clostridium perfringens*), act to kill host cells. These toxins are released during active microbial proliferation within infected tissue, which defines this zone. In contrast, zones like "contamination" or "irritation" involve non-active exposure without established pathogen replication. Thus, bacteriocidal activity occurs specifically in the zone of infection.
