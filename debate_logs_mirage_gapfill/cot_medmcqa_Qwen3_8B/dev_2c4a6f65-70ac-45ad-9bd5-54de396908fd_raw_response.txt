**Answer:** D: *Pneumocystis jirovecii*  

*Rationale:* Dimorphic fungi switch between yeast and mold forms depending on environmental temperature, with *Histoplasma*, *Blastomyces*, and *Paracoccidioides (e.g., P. marneffi)* exhibiting this behavior. In contrast, *Pneumocystis jirovecii* remains strictly as an obligate intracellular protozoan-like organism without morphological dimorphism; it does not undergo phase changes like yeasts to molds under varying temperatures. Therefore, it is not classified as a dimorphic fungus.
