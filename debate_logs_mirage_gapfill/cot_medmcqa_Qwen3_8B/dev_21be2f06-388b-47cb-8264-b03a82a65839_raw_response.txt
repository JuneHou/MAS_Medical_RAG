**Answer:** A: Posteriorly downward  

**Reasoning:** In long face syndrome (characterized by increased lower facial height), the maxilla is typically retrognathic or vertically elongated. This results in an overall posterior displacement of the palatal plane relative to the dental arches, leading to a postero-downward orientation when viewed from lateral views. Therefore, the palatal plane shifts posteriorly and downward due to skeletal changes associated with this condition.
