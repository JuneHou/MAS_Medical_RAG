Non-paralytic polio, also known as asymptomatic or mild cases, typically presents with symptoms that do not lead to motor neuron damage resulting in paralysis. Features such as absent deep tendon reflexes (which would indicate nerve involvement), head drop due to weakness of neck muscles, and nuchal rigidity associated with meningitis can be seen in more severe forms but are less likely in non-paralytic presentations where there is no significant muscle weakness leading to these signs.

Answer Choice A The correct option here is A - Absent deep tendon reflexes since this feature suggests neuromuscular involvement which does not occur in typical non-paralytic polio. 

The other options like B (head drop), C(nuchal rigidity) may sometimes present if meningeal irritation occurs even without full blown paralysis. D(Knee-heel test negativity) might be observed secondary to weak lower limb function but isn't specific for paralytic vs nonparalytic polio distinction on its own. But among them all, absence of deep tendon reflexes stands out uniquely indicative of neurological impairment beyond what's expected in true "non-paralytic" polio scenarios.
