**Answer:** D: Hyperphosphatemia  

Renal osteodystrophy, which results from chronic kidney disease (CKD), commonly features secondary hyperparathyroidism due to impaired vitamin D activation and phosphate retention. This leads to elevated serum phosphorus levels (hyperphosphatemia), distinguishing it from nutritional or genetic forms of osteomalacia that typically present with hypophosphatemia.
