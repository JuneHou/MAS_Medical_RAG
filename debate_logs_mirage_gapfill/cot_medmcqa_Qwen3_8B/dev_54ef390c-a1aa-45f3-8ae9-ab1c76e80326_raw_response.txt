The key to pulp necrosis due to bacterial infection lies primarily with how effectively the bacteria can establish an environment conducive for their survival and proliferation. Among these factors, the virulence of the bacteria plays a critical role as it determines whether they can outcompete or directly damage host tissues rapidly.

My answer is **B** (Virulence of bacteria). To clarify further, while host resistance also impacts outcome, more virulent pathogens can overcome less robust defenses quickly leading to faster tissue destruction and subsequent necrosis if not contained early on.
