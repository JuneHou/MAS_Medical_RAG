Triphenyltetrazolium chloride (TTC) is used as an indicator for recent ischemic changes in cardiac tissue during autopsies or post-mortem examinations because it reduces to red formazan crystals when exposed to viable mitochondria but does not reduce in necrotic areas which remain white. Since myocardial infarction involves the death of heart muscle cells leading to irreversible damage and thus no reduction reaction can occur in these regions, they would appear white after staining.

Answer Choice A: White The correct color that TTC-stained myocardium affected by myocardial infarction will show is **White**. This indicates irreversibly damaged tissues where mitochondrial function has ceased, preventing the formation of the reduced formazan product visible elsewhere as red stain.
