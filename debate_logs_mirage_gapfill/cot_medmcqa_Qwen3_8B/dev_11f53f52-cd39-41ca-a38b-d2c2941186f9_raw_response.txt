**Answer:** B: Mandibular plane  
**Clinical reasoning:** The mandibular plane is commonly used in cephalometric analysis because it represents the anteroposterior relationship between the maxilla and mandible, allowing for accurate assessment of skeletal growth patterns. It serves as a reference line when evaluating vertical and horizontal changes over time during development. This makes it ideal for tracking growth changes through longitudinal studies.
