**C**: Contraction is initiated by calcium binding to troponin.  
Calcium released from the sarcoplasmic reticulum binds to troponin on the thin filament, causing a conformational change that moves tropomyosin away from the myosin-binding sites on actin, allowing cross-bridge formation and muscle contraction. This is the key initiating event in skeletal muscle excitation-contraction coupling.
