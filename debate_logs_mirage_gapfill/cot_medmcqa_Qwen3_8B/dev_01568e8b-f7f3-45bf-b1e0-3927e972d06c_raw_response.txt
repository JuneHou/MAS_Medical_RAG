**Answer:** B: Posteriorly  

**Clinical Reasoning:** The posterior portion of the mouth has more stable anatomy (with well-defined ridges), allowing for better primary seating. Seating the tray posteriorly ensures proper alignment with the posterior border, which helps prevent undercuts and improves accuracy when placing the anterior portions later during impression-taking. This step is critical to avoid distortion and ensure an accurate final impression.
