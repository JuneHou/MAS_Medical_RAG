The evidence provided supports the correctness of claiming that hypokalemia does not show ECG findings identical to those observed in hyperkalemia. While both conditions affect cardiac electrical activity, their manifestations differ significantly. For example, while hyperkalemia can lead to tall T waves and peaked P waves, hypokalemia tends to cause flattened T waves and shortening of the QT interval. These differences arise because hyperkalemia disrupts potassium balance inside and outside cells, affecting repolarization dynamics more dramatically compared to low potassium levels.
