Answer:

**Answer:** B: Cocaine  

**Reasoning:** Cocaine causes sympathomimetic effects including agitation, dilated pupils (mydriasis), tachycardia, sweating, and gastrointestinal disturbances like diarrhea. These symptoms align closely with cocaine toxicity. In contrast, heroin typically causes sedation, not agitation or diaphoresis; cannabis may cause drowsiness or confusion; ecstasy can lead to hyperthermia but less commonly presents with prominent autonomic stimulation such as pronounced dilation and sweating alongside diarrhea. The constellation supports acute cocaine intoxication.
