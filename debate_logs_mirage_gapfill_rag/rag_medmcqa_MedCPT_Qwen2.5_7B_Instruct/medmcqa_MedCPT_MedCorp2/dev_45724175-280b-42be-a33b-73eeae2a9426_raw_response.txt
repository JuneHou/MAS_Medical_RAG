The key information here pertains to the landmarks in root-end filling during endodontics. Specifically, the apical foramen marks the boundary between the pulp chamber and periapical tissues, serving as the endpoint for root-canal preparation and sealing materials like gutta-percha. Therefore:
\boxed{\text{B}}
The correct pure histological landmark for terminating cleaning, shaping, and obturation in endodontic therapy is the **Apical Foramen** (\boxed{\text{B}}).
This landmark signifies the physiological limit beyond which no further instrumentation should be conducted to avoid extrusion of debris or instruments into adjacent tissue spaces. It demarcates the transition zone between the root canal space and surrounding periodontal ligament and alveolar bone. Thus, identifying and respecting the apical foramen ensures proper completion of endodontic treatments without compromising neighboring soft and hard tissues.
