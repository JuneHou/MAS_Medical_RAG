The laboratory investigations mentioned include increased Prothrombin Time (PT), activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT), decreased fibrinogen levels, and elevated Fragment-D-dimer (FDPs).
- **Increased PT** suggests prolonged clotting time due to reduced amounts of coagulation factors.
- **Increased aPTT** indicates prolongation of intrinsic pathway activation, often seen in conditions like DIC but less commonly than PT elevation alone.
- **Decreased fibrinogen** points towards consumption or dysfunction leading to hypofibrinogenaemia.
- **Elevated FDPs** indicate ongoing fibrin degradation, consistent with active coagulation followed by dissolution.
Given that DIC involves widespread microvascular thrombosis and subsequent consumptive coagulopathy resulting from extensive intravascular fibrin deposition and lysis, **elevated FDPs** would reflect the balance between excessive clot formation and rapid lysis characteristic of DIC. Therefore:

This aligns well with the hallmark feature of disseminated intravascular coagulation involving significant fibrin turnover and thus elevated fragment-degrading products (FDPs). The other tests might suggest different types of coagulopathies without necessarily capturing the dynamic process typical of DIC. Thus, FDP measurement stands out as particularly indicative of the ongoing coagulative changes observed in DIC.
