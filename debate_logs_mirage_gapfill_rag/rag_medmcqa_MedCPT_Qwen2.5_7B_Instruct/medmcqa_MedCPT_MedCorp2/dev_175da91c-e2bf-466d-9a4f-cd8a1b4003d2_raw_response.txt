Title: Metabolism of Red Blood Cells Under Fasting Conditions - Focus on Energy Sources
Abstract: In the context of prolonged fasting, red blood cells (RBCs) predominantly utilize glucose derived from glycolysis rather than other metabolic intermediates such as alanine, ketone bodies, or fatty acids. Despite their ability to oxidize certain lipids, RBCs lack key enzymes required for beta-oxidation of longer chain fatty acids making these less accessible as direct fuel sources compared to simple carbohydrates like glucose.
