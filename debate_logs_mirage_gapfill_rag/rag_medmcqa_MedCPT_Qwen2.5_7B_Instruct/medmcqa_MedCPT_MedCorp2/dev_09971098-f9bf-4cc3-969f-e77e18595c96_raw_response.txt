Based on the retrieved evidence, we see that laparoscopy and hysteroscopy offer advantages particularly in terms of providing a direct view of anatomical structures allowing for precise diagnosis and potentially treating conditions affecting fertility. There isn't compelling evidence showing superior outcomes favoring one technique over the other except perhaps minor cost differences noted where laparoscopy might be slightly cheaper. Importantly, for a patient whose main issue appears to be tubal blockages, laparoscopy allows for attempts at corrective measures that IVF would circumvent entirely - meaning if the tubes were opened during the procedure and resulted in natural conception, subsequent treatments wouldn’t need to proceed down the IVF route. Therefore, given current findings, continuing efforts focused on trying to restore tubal functionality through minimally-invasive means seems warranted unless absolutely impossible or highly unlikely due to severe pathology.
