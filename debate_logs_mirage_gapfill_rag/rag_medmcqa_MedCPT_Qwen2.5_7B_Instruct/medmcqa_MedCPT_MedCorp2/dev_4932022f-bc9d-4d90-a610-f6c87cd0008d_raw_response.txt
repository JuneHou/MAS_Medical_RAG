While GSDI does involve significant hepatomegaly and abnormal liver biochemistry, particularly in relation to glycolytic enzyme deficiencies leading to intracellular accumulation of glycogen, the key distinguishing pathological findings in GSDI—such as prominent hepatomegaly and elevated serum transaminases—are not emphasized strongly enough relative to the current description focusing heavily on multinucleated giant cells and hemopoiesis.
Moreover, the reference material highlights that GSDI often presents with characteristic biochemical elevations (mainly AST and ALT) yet doesn't correlate strongly with prominent proliferative activities involving multinuclear giant cells unless secondary to massive cellular dysfunction or degeneration due to severe substrate overload. Additionally, though GSDI poses notable risks of developing liver adenomas and malignant transformations, its histopathological hallmarks do not predominantly highlight substantial numbers of multinucleated giant cells nor profound haemopoietic elements as prominently as suggested herein.
